What we would have changed

If we had realised the challenges involved in developing, managing, and uploading databases to a website we may have considered a different topic to do our project on. Having a topic that didn't rely so heavily on databases would've saved our group a lot of time in the long run, allowing us to focus more on the quality of each feature. However, due our lack of database knowledge, this assignment taught us a lot about PHP and databases. For that reason rather than abandoning databases all together, we may have just picked a project that wouldn't be as dependant on databases to function.

Throughout development which features we would implement frequently changed. As did our roles and which members worked on which features. Because of our rapidly changing plans some members had more work than others, and so we frequently switched and swapped members around to balance workload. If we were to do the project again we would've dedicated more time to developing a concrete plan before hand that would've allowed our group to be more focused on each feature.

Was our timetable realistic

As reached the end of the semester and more and more due dates for different subjects grew closer, our team realised we wouldn't have as much time to dedicate to our project as we initially outlined in our estimation plan. Our time table ended up being unrealistic as we assigned more time for some features than we were realistically capable of doing. Several features were overestimated however, and actually took longer to develop than outlined.

These differences were mainly because of our inexperience with certain aspects of each feature such as databases, leading to under/overestimate aspects we wern't as knowledgeable about. We also started the actual development of our features a little later than intially planned. This lead to a few innacurate estimations as we hadn't actually got our hands dirty with the development of that feature yet to really grasp how short/long its development would take.
